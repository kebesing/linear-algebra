Recall the four fundamental subspaces associated to an \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\). The column space, \(\mathrm{col}(A)\), spanned by all of the columns of \(A\). This is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^m\). The row space, \(\mathrm{row}(A)\), spanned by all of the rows of \(A\). This is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\). This also happens to be the column space of \(A^T\). The nullspace (or kernel), \(\mathrm{null}(A)\), consisting of all those vectors \(x\) for which \(Ax=0\). This is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\). The left nullspace, which is just the nullspace of \(A^T\). This is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^m\).
