This makes \(A\) into a kind of function. (We will use the synonyms mapping or transformation, too.) For every vector \(v\) of size \(n\), the matrix \(A\) allows us to compute a new vector \(T_A(v) = Av\) of size \(m\). This is the basic example of what professionals call a linear transformation.\begin{align*} \mathbb{R}^n & \xrightarrow[]{T_A} \mathbb{R}^m\\ v & \xmapsto[]{\phantom{T_A}} Av. \end{align*} One of our long term goals is to learn to think about the geometry of linear algebra from this viewpoint, too.
