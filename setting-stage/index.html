Setting the Stage

Linear Algebra, Spring 2016

Theron J Hitchman

Directions

  • Get into groups of 3-4
  • Introduce yourself to your group
  • For each of the questions that follow:
    1. Think about a possible answer on your own
    2. Discuss the answer with your group
    3. Share a summary of the group's discussion

Question One:

What are the goals of a University Education?

Question Two:

How does a person learn something new?

Question Three:

What can you reasonably expect to remember from your courses 20 years from now?

Question Four:

What is the value of making mistakes in the learning process?

Question Five:

How can we create a safe environment where risk taking is encouraged and productive failure is valued?

Productive Failure

Any creative endeavor is built on the ash heap of failure. --- Mike Starbird

The Big Picture

Claims

  • An education must prepare a student to ask and explore questions in contexts that do not yet exist. That is, we need individuals capable of tackling problems they have never encountered and to ask questions no one has yet imagined.
  • If I really want you to be independent, inquisitive, & persistent, then I need to provide you with the means to acquire these skills.

Lofty Goals

  • Transition students from consumers to producers!
  • I want to provide the opportunity for a transformative experience.
